In March 2025 I’ve bought a [[workout loop]] (also known as “resistance loop”) – I can’t tell what exactly I bought, since I got *some* off-brand workout loop from my local shopping center just to try it out and without too much thinking – and I *loved* it.

I believed that I liked the experience so much because of my [[interoception]] issues, which are pretty common among [[neurodivergent people]]:

> **Interoception** is the collection of [senses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sense#Other_internal_sensations_and_perceptions "Sense") providing information to the organism about the internal state of the body.
> 
> – [[DRAFT Interoception (Wikipedia)|Interoception (Wikipedia)]]

When I exercise without resistance loop, I don’t feel much impact. I might be tired, but otherwise I don’t feel any signs of progress (which is absolutely natural, since sport isn’t meant to give results right away), and I get bored extremely quickly. On the contrary, when I used my workout loop, I literally felt my muscles stretching, and I couldn’t get enough of this feeling. It felt like heavy lifting, but without the “heavy” part.

However, I absolutely forgot that I need to warm up before any substantial exercise, so after a few days I’ve earned myself a back pain. It turns out that “receiving feedback which you like” doesn’t mean “the activity itself is good for you”. Make sure to double-check required safety measures beforehand.
